Safety During Specific Disasters

  • Earthquake
    • During: Drop to hands and knees, cover head/neck under a table or against an interior wall, hold on until shaking stops (30-60 seconds typically).
    • Avoid elevators, glass, and exterior walls. If outside, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
    • After: Expect aftershocks; check for gas leaks (smell or hissing), turn off main if safe. Wear shoes to avoid debris.
  • Flood

  • Tornado

    • Use a mattress, pillows, or helmet to shield your head. If in a car, abandon it for a ditch‚Äîlie flat, cover head.

  • Hurricane

    • Post-fire: Check for embers in roofs/gutters; avoid ash piles (toxic chemicals).

Post-Disaster Survival

  • Water: If tap water is unsafe, boil it for 1 minute (rolling boil) or add 8 drops of unscented bleach per gallon, wait 30 minutes. Filter murky water through cloth first.
  • Food: Use refrigerated food within 4 hours without power; cook perishables first. Discard cans dented or touched by floodwater.
  • Shelter: Avoid buildings with cracked walls or shifted foundations. Use tarps, tents, or car interiors if needed; insulate with blankets or dry leaves.
  • Hygiene: Wash hands with soap or sanitizer after touching debris. Treat cuts immediately to prevent infection.
